Golden Rules of Prompting

The 6 Commandments

16

Rule 1

Be Clear, Not Polite

‚ùå "Hi there, hope you are well. Could you maybe please help me write a..."

‚úÖ "Draft a 500-word article about..."

AI doesn't have feelings. Focus on clarity and verbs.

17

Rule 2

One Prompt = One Goal

Don't overload the system.

‚ùå Mixed: "Create an image of a cat for me, then write a caption for it, and then give me code for a website portfolio."

‚úÖ Focused: "Write a caption for a photo of a cat." (Do the rest in separate steps)

18

Rule 3

No Contradictions

‚ùå Contradicting ‚úÖ Clear
"Strictly preserve face AND replace face" "Replace face, keep background"
"Keep it short but explain everything in detail" "Give a concise 3-sentence summary"
"Be creative but follow this exact template" "Use this template, fill in creative content"
19

Rule 4

Use STRICT / ONLY

When requirements are non-negotiable, use capital letters for emphasis.

"STRICTLY do not use emojis."

"Output ONLY the code. No explanation."

20

Rule 5

Speak If You Can't Type

üéôÔ∏è

Voice input allows for "yapping" ‚Äì rambling your thoughts.

Let the AI organize your messy spoken thoughts into a structured prompt.

21

Rule 6

"Final Prompt Only"

A meta-tactic.

"Refine my request into the perfect prompt for ChatGPT..."

Prevents the AI from giving you a lecture on how it improved it.

Powerful Techniques

Level Up Your Game

23

Technique 1

Persona / Role

"Act as a..." or "You are an expert..."

  • Marketing: "Act as a Senior Brand Strategist with 20 years experience."
  • Coding: "You are a Principal Software Engineer at Google."
  • Health: "You are a professional Nutritionist."

This primes the model to access specific subsets of its training data.

Technique 2

Technique 6

"Analyze My Style"

Teach AI to write like YOU.

Step 1: Paste 3 of your previous emails/blogs.

Step 2: "Now write a new email about [topic] using that exact style."

29

For Coders

The "Cursor" Planning Hack

For AI Coding Editors (Cursor, Windsurf, Copilot):

Step 1: "Make a plan for [feature] in @file"

Step 2: "Review the plan. If I missed edge cases, update it."

Step 3: "Now execute the plan step-by-step."

Reduces bugs by forcing the AI to "think" before "coding".
